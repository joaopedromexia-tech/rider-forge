How to Create a Technical Rider for a Band

Complete step-by-step guide for creating professional technical riders

Step 1: Gather Basic Information

Start by collecting all the essential information about your band and the event:

  • Band/Artist Name: Official name and any variations
  • Contact Information: Tour manager, sound engineer, and production contacts
  • Event Details: Date, venue, type of performance
  • Band Size: Number of musicians and instruments
  • Performance Duration: Set length and number of sets
Pro Tip: Include multiple contact methods (phone, email) and specify the best time to reach each person.

Step 2: List Audio Equipment Requirements

Detail all audio equipment needs for your performance:

Microphones

  • Vocal microphones (specify models like Shure SM58, Sennheiser e935)
  • Instrument microphones (kick drum, snare, guitar amps, etc.)
  • Specialty microphones (condenser, ribbon, etc.)

Consoles and Mixing

  • FOH (Front of House) console preferences
  • Monitor console requirements
  • Minimum channel count needed
  • Digital vs analog preferences

Monitoring Systems

  • Monitor wedges or in-ear monitors (IEMs)
  • Side fills and drum fills
  • Monitor mix requirements for each musician

PA System

  • Main PA system specifications
  • Subwoofer requirements
  • Front fill needs
  • Power and rigging requirements

Step 3: Create Stage Plot

A stage plot is a visual representation of your stage setup:

Essential Elements

  • Stage Dimensions: Width, depth, and height
  • Musician Positions: Where each band member will be located
  • Equipment Placement: Amplifiers, monitors, instruments
  • Power Requirements: Electrical outlets and power distribution
  • Access Points: Loading areas and stage entrances

Technical Details

  • Monitor wedge positions and angles
  • Microphone placement and stands
  • Cable routing and snake positions
  • Backline equipment (amps, keyboards, drums)
Important: Include measurements and be specific about equipment models and brands when possible.

Step 4: Add Technical Specifications

Include detailed technical requirements:

Input List

  • Channel assignments for each instrument
  • Microphone types and models
  • DI box requirements
  • Phantom power needs

Monitor Mixes

  • Individual monitor mix requirements
  • Stereo vs mono preferences
  • IEM vs wedge preferences
  • Click track and backing track needs

Additional Requirements

  • Lighting specifications
  • Backstage amenities
  • Catering and hospitality needs
  • Load-in and soundcheck schedules

Step 5: Review and Export

Finalize your technical rider:

Review Checklist

  • All contact information is current and complete
  • Equipment specifications are accurate and detailed
  • Stage plot is clear and includes all necessary elements
  • Technical requirements are realistic and achievable
  • Document is professional and easy to read

Distribution

  • Export as PDF for easy sharing
  • Send to venue and production team in advance
  • Include in booking contracts
  • Keep digital and printed copies
Best Practice: Send your technical rider at least 2-3 weeks before the event to allow time for preparation and any necessary adjustments.

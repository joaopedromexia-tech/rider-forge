What is a Tech Rider?

A technical rider (or tech rider) is a detailed document that outlines all the technical requirements for a live performance or event. It's essential for sound engineers, production teams, and venues to ensure everything runs smoothly.

As a sound engineer, creating a comprehensive tech rider is crucial for communicating your technical needs clearly and professionally.

5 Steps to Create a Professional Tech Rider

1

Plan Your Structure

Start by organizing your tech rider into logical sections: general information, PA system requirements, console specifications, monitor mixes, input lists, and final notes. This structure ensures nothing is missed.

2

Use Professional Templates

Leverage industry-standard templates that include all necessary sections. Professional templates save time and ensure your tech rider meets industry expectations.

3

Specify Equipment Requirements

Detail all equipment specifications including brands, models, quantities, and technical requirements. Be specific about input/output needs, power requirements, and any special considerations.

4

Include Monitor Mixes and Input Lists

Provide detailed monitor mix specifications and comprehensive input lists. This information is crucial for the front-of-house and monitor engineers to set up correctly.

5

Review and Export

Double-check all specifications for accuracy and completeness. Export your tech rider to PDF format for easy sharing and professional presentation.

Best Practices for Tech Rider Creation

Be Specific and Clear

  • • Use exact model numbers and specifications
  • • Include quantities for all equipment
  • • Specify power requirements and connections
  • • Provide clear input/output configurations

Professional Presentation

  • • Use consistent formatting and layout
  • • Include contact information
  • • Add version numbers and dates
  • • Use professional language and terminology
